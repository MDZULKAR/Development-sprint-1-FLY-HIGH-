REFERENCING


Referencing refers to the practice of acknowledging the sources of your ideas when you are writing assignments, examinations, reports and other material associated with your unit of study.

Clear and consistent referencing shows you can demonstrate your understanding of the topic, support your ideas with research and avoid accusations of plagiarism.

Discover more about common university referencing styles as well as steps for referencing throughout your work. We also have information on referencing software and other helpful tools.

This site provides advice on the appropriate referencing style for your unit and gives guidelines for using each of the referencing styles..

Why reference?

Showing the breadth of your research

Strengthening your academic argument

Allowing readers to verify your information.

What is plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting, without any form of acknowledgment, the ideas or words of another writer as if they were your own. This is more than just another form of cheating. It is literary theft. It is stealing someone else's work..

Quoting, paraphrasing and summarising

Quoting, paraphrasing and summarizing are similar in that they allow a writer to incorporate another writer's work into his or her own work. However, they are different in the methods of application.

Acknowledging sources in text
